# Author’s Note

**Michael Vail ‘24**

*A Sight For Sore Eyes*

#

[Author's Note: To all my dedicated readers, I hope you'll forgive my absence in recent issues of *The Spire*. I broke my pencil and could not buy a new one to write with until Dining Services paid me enough to afford it. It's been difficult given the hospital bills. If you're curious, the incident happened when I had just gotten off a shift at Kimball. I figured I'd have a go at the bouncy house they set up recently for Philanthropy Day. (For all who don't know, Philanthropy Day is the day the school runs out of money to provide for us students (except for their massive endowment (the number is somewhere in the quadrillions, I think), which we won't talk about right now). As a result, they have to ask alumni to keep paying them after graduation, and some of them actually do.) This year, in honor of the selfless donors who gave their salaries for our future, the college used their money to rent a bouncy house. I guess I backflipped a little too ambitiously in praise of our generous donors, and after a minute or two of nostalgic-kindergarten-birthday-party-kinda-vibes-glee, I landed terribly on my wrist, causing my femur to break in six different places. So as you can imagine, I haven't been able to write very well since. And as I mentioned, I haven't had a pencil to write with. I lied about Dining Services finally paying me enough to buy a new one; I stole this one from the library.]
